ARTS 102

online

As a reminder, these weekly community design challenges are all about sharing ideas and explorations with each other. More importantly, they are about cultivating your own artistic sensibilities. They encourage you to be curious about the world around you and to look with fresh eyes at things that seem familiar.

For this week's challenge, I would like you to document something that fascinates you. What are the little things about the world that you find neat; things that capture your attention and make you pause in your day. They can be things others find fascinating or could be things that everyone thinks you are weird for liking. It doesn't matter, no judgment here.

Some things that I find fascinating: sunlight streaming through a window onto a wall or floor, weeds growing through a crack in the sidewalk or blacktop, worn wood (especially if there is paint peeling), the architecture of books stacked up in a jagged way, the little "hook" on top of soft-serve ice-cream cones. What about you?

I would like for you to pick one topic or theme and take five pictures that capture your fascination. Spread this out, don't just take five shots of the same thing. Go explore and find multiple examples. This means you may have to go outside or even travel to different places. This means it might take more than one session playing with your camera. That's OK. The point is to go out and actually look at the world.

ASSIGNMENT SUBMISSION

To turn in your assignment, take your pictures and post them on the discussion forum for everyone to see. Give us a short intro so we know what we are looking at.

I'm not requiring anyone to post comments on each others work, but feel free to compliment and support each other. It is that sense of community that we're trying to build. Do what you can to participate in the community conversation.

ASSIGNMENT EVALUATION

I'm not going to grade these. I'm just going to enjoy them. I might give some feedback. Mostly, I just want you to make something. You get full points for making and sharing them.
