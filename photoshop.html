GETTING PHOTOSHOP

You will be using Adobe Photoshop to complete the design assignments in this class. Using your LCC email, you can get a subscription for about $10 a month.

If you cannot purchase Photoshop (or do not want to) a wonderful alternative to Photoshop is Affinity Designer. A free 90-day trial is available during the pandemic which will be enough to get you through this course. There is an iPad app version as well.

If you have decent bandwidth, you can also use Photopea, an online browser-based alternative that looks and acts very much like Photoshop. You can even create and edit in Photoshop's native .psd file format. It is a free tool, but if you want to add more features, you can subscribe for about $3 a month.

USING PHOTOSHOP

Photoshop is a beast of a program. It can be scary when you first open it up. It can be even scarier when you run into a problem and don't know how to fix it. In a classroom setting it is easy for me to show you how to do something or how to dig out of a hole. Unfortunately, I can't easily do that for you in an online-only setting. There may be some frustrations involved, but we will work through it together.

Each assignment will have basic instructions on how to complete the assigned tasks. The instructions aren't overly deep or complex, although they should be enough to help you get started.

Here are additional resources that may help with Photoshop:

  • You can send me an email (Communication tab) through the class site to ask for help. I will try to get back to you as soon as I can. This isn't the best option.
  • You can send an email to our Learning Assistant(Communication tab) through the class site to ask for help. This is a better option.
  • There is a discussion forum (use the link or go to the Communication tab) where you can ask for help from me and from your classmates. This is a better option because your classmates may be able to answer quicker than me. Plus, someone may have already asked the same question and gotten an answer.
  • Photoshop Course: Tools In-Depth is a great youtube series that shows you how quickly how to use the tools.
  • Lynda.com is a wonderful resource for video tutorials. There is a subscription fee to access the videos, however access is available to Capital Area District Libraries (CADL) members for free with a valid library card.
  • If nothing else, this Learn Photoshop post gives you links to many more learning resources.
