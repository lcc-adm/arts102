applying the concepts

ARTS 102

online

The design exercises are meant as explorations of how the principles and elements can be used to develop the projects. You will be creating different pieces exploring specific ideas. The hope is that by focusing on the different ideas that you will discover the best way to illustrate your projects.

These are meant to be studies and explorations for the projects. Quick and rough low-fidelity explorations of how you could go about making your final images. These are not meant to be beautiful or perfect, they are simply meant to explore possibilities. This is a process of curiosity and play, experimentation and discovery. The lessons learned here will be used to build the actual digital collages for the project.

Think of this as working in your sketchbook as you plan out your design.

These images will help you to understand the idea better than words.

visual exploration

Forms these explorations can take include, but are not limited to:

  • play with arrangement of elements through physical collage of elements
  • draw out different arrangement possibilities
  • make color studies of compositional ideas
  • emulate ideas from collage research
  • experiment with different art media like markers, paint, crayons
  • print out and color on top of digital images
  • cut up and assemble construction paper shapes

analysis of the exploration

Written analysis of what was made and what was learned. Your writing doesn't need to be formal or very long. I'm not going to worry about spelling and grammar (although you should). Just tell me (and yourself) what you did and what you learned. Please include vocab related to the design principles.

ASSIGNMENT SUBMISSION & EVALUATION

Your explorations will probably take different forms. That's OK. Simply scan or photograph them and upload as a group of files to our shared Google folder. Your written work can be created as a Google Doc and placed in the same folder.

Although there may be some overlap in the files, an individual grade will be given for each of the principles.

unity

  • 3 pts: at least three studies / explorations for how to achieve visual unity
  • 3 pts: at least three studies / explorations for how to achieve conceptual unity
  • 2 pts: written analysis

emphasis

  • 3 pts: at least three studies for how to create focal points and accents for visual unity design
  • 3 pts: at least three studies for how to create focal points and accents for conceptual unity design
  • 2 pts: written analysis

balance

  • 3 pts: at least three composition studies specifically for symmetry
  • 3 pts: at least three composition studies specifically for asymmetry
  • 2 pts: written analysis
