As a reminder, these weekly community design challenges are all about sharing ideas and explorations with each other. More importantly, they are about cultivating your own artistic sensibilities. They encourage you to be curious about the world around you and to look with fresh eyes at things that seem familiar.

An important part of being an artist is connecting with your world, connecting with the muse, connecting with your curiosity. As artists, we deal directly with true experience and capture our adventures in some sort of tangible form to share with others. Our recorded experiences communicate the adventure and can take on the form of paintings, music, sketches, drawings, doodles, patterns, rhythms, dance, film, animation, etc. The experiences themselves can be physical adventures, emotional explorations, conceptual play, etc. Often times, the artistic experiential journey is one that delves into the unknown. It is an act of personal discovery.

Your assignment for this artistâ€™s date is to simply BE CURIOUS! I want you to go discover something new about your world.

IDEAS

These can be as complex or as simple as you want to make it.

You can go hang out at a book store or library and look up something in a book. Technically, you could use the internet to find out new facts, but how boring is that? Force yourself out into the world. It isn't that scary.

This can also be experiential. You could go somewhere you've never been before. Take a drive down that one road you always pass and wonder about. Maybe go to a restaurant you've never been to before or visit your favorite restaurant and try something new off the menu. You get the idea.

A fun example. A student many years ago decided to try out pistachios. She had always been scared of eating them because they are green and who wants to eat green food? Her grandma always made pistachio pudding and it always looked like green slime to her. Why would you eat that? So this girl went to the bulk-food store and bought $1 worth of pistachios. She sat in her car, built up her courage, and tried them. Turns out they are amazing. She came to class with five pounds of pistachios to share with everyone. So much fun.

NOTES

Obligatory Warning: this in no way gives you permission to go do something illegal, immoral, or potentially dangerous for your assignment. Please do not get arrested or sent to the hospital because you are doing something stupid.

ASSIGNMENT SUBMISSION

To turn in your assignment, you will simply write a post on the discussion forum (Communication tab) telling us what you did and how it went. It would be great if you have some tangible, visual documentation to share. It could be a photo or a drawing or even a visual poem. Let the experience inspire you.

I'm not requiring anyone to post comments on each others work, but feel free to compliment and support each other. It is that sense of community that we're trying to build. Do what you can to participate in the community conversation.

ASSIGNMENT EVALUATION

I'm not going to grade these. I'm just going to enjoy them. I might give some feedback. Mostly, I just want you to make something. You get full points for making and sharing them.
