ARTS 102

online

Back in the 90s, zines were the rage in counter-culture self-publishing. You created your own magazine and ran off copies at the local Kinkos. It was a way to spread information before the internet. It was a way to get your voice out there. Underground bands and skaters made good use of them, as did conspiracy theorists and Riot Grrrl feminists. Even your old teacher was known for some of his zines (and homemade postcards).

One of my favorite inspirational people is Austin Kleon. He's made a lot of mini-zines. I want you to make one in his style this week. They are meant to be quick and loose and not at all precious. They are an opportunity to play with an idea or to express an opinion. They are a chance to be creative in a way that isn't too caught up in being perfect. Seems like a good thing to get you back into the creative flow.

I'm almost completely open to whatever you want to make for your zine. Create a mini-comic book. Write a poem. List what you've been doing duringlockdown. List what you wish you could have been doing during lockdown. Illustrate your favorite foods. Give yourself permission to play. It could be funny or sad or silly or meaningful. Use it to complain about the plague or dream of a different world. It is your chance to say what you want. Just make something interesting that has something to say about life and your perception of it.

I'm also open to styles and techniques. Collage bits and pieces. Draw in different ways. Use a typewriter for text (if you have access to one). Paint with watercolor or coffee. Use material and sew them. Use stickers in weird ways. You get the idea. Just play!

how to make a zine

All it takes to make this type of zine is a sheet of paper and a pair scissors. Actually, the scissors are not even a requirement. You can just rip the paper a bit instead.

Austin Kleon has a video on how to make one. Here's a low-budget instruction sheet. I'm sure you can find more alternatives online.

If you would prefer to work digitally, here's a Photoshop template to use. The text layer is just to show you how the spreads work. You can turn that layer off. For those of you who don't have Photoshop, here's a flat template with the spreads and a flat template without the spreads to actually use. When done, print, fold, and cut.

inspiration

For inspiration, check out the #zines tag on Instagram.

Or maybe do an internet search for art zines or collage zines or even cartoon art zines for inspiration. No matter what, you will find that the focus is on ideas and not necessarily artistic quality. Of course, make yours as awesome as you can!

ASSIGNMENT SUBMISSION

To turn in your assignment, take some pictures of your zine. Show covers and pages. Post them on the discussion forum for everyone to see. Give us a short intro so we know what we are looking at.

I'm not requiring anyone to post comments on each others work, but feel free to compliment and support each other. It is that sense of community that we're trying to build. Do what you can to participate in the community conversation.

ASSIGNMENT EVALUATION

I'm not going to grade these zines. I'm just going to enjoy them. I might give some feedback. Mostly, I just want you to make something. You get full points for making and sharing them.
